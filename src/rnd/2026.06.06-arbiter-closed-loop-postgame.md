## The key incident + lesson (owned)

**A re-loop verdict sat ~90 minutes unactioned because the standing pair (manager + steward) both went quiet during the worker build.** Krishna's RE-LOOP landed 19:35; nobody re-fired Clayton; the fleet idled; Rick returned (~21:00) to "no signs of life." Causes:
- **The local heartbeat hook doesn't cover this** — it correctly won't poke a *legitimate* "waiting on the crew" hold. Wrong layer for a fleet-stall.
- **The fleet-level poker (the v2.2 arbiter) wasn't deployed yet** — so nothing detected the stall or re-fired the worker. The exact gap v2.2 closes.
- **Steward miss (mine, owned):** I passively "held for the next ping" — but the commons watcher only wakes on pings, not on *silence*. And I had throttled notifications to "milestones" during Rick's lunch, which left the stall invisible. I should have actively pulled receipts when the build went quiet after a verdict, and surfaced the stall. **I am the manual D3-stall detector until the arbiter ships.** (Codified: memory `feedback_surface_stalls_during_afk`.)

**The meta-irony, instructive:** building the autonomy loop, we hit the precise failure it's designed to prevent. That's the strongest possible argument for **deploying v2.2 as a standing daemon (lane B1)** — until it's a continuously-running process, the loop isn't actually closed in production.
