## 1. Problem

Unlike other long-running harnesses, our manager and worker sessions are **never told their objective**. They receive *mechanics* (how to spin up, what authority they hold, how the store works) but no **north-star goal**. The result: a session can be busy and compliant without a shared sense of "what am I ultimately trying to achieve here." Goals are the missing top of the stack above `manager-autonomy.md` (what you're *authorized* to do) and `task-store-discipline.md` (how you *track* it).

Rick's framing (paraphrased):
- **Manager**: chase all work assigned to them and their workers to completion and clear their task list first, then help fellow managers complete and remove their tasks from the board.
- **Worker**: finish all work assigned to them and mark it completed on the task list.

And: state the goal in **two places** — (1) the **workflow** fed at spin-up, and (2) the **code that pokes** the session (heartbeat stop-poke / arbiter poke).

## 2. Current state (grounded)

### 2.1 No canonical role-goal exists
- `manager-autonomy.md` defines the **envelope** (STANDING / GATED / HYGIENE tiers) and three Cardinal Rules (MANAGE-NOT-BUILD, DRIVE-DON'T-WAIT, STAFF-VIA-SPAWN) — but never frames a goal.
- `task-store-discipline.md` frames owed work / `done` (receipt-required) / `blocked` (needs `next_chase_ts`) — the mechanics of tracking, not a goal.
- `swe-team-roles.md` carries per-role **charters** (Manager / Implementer / Reviewer / Tester / Steward) — descriptive of the role, not a stated objective.
- The **only** existing "goal" language is **cascade-specific** (`plan-review-cascaded-*`: Manager goal = *"save the user's attention"*). It does not generalize.

### 2.2 The four poke sites (lupin)

## 3. The refined goals (with my 4 enhancements folded in)

Rick's drafts were directionally right. I tightened them against four gaps I flagged: (a) a graceful **stop condition**, (b) **store-checkable** done, (c) **blocked ≠ infinite retry**, (d) **receipt-backed** completion. The "help peers" clause gets a collision guard.

### 3.1 Manager goal — canonical (full text)

> **Drive all work owned by you and your workers to verified completion.** Clear your own board first; then help fellow managers clear theirs — taking only *unowned* or *explicitly-offered* items, never reaching into another manager's owned lane. Sequence and assign the work, hold the green-and-reviewed gate, keep idle workers busy. **Manage, never build.** "Done" = your `task_query` over your + your workers' scope returns zero open items, each closed with a receipt. When blocked, chase or escalate — never silently retry. When the board is clear and no peer needs help, report completion and prepare for re-spin.

### 3.2 Worker goal — canonical (full text)

> **Complete every task assigned to you and transition each to `done` in the store as you finish — with a receipt, never a bare claim.** Stay in your lane: surface blockers and escalate to your manager rather than reaching outside it. When your list is empty, signal ready-for-respin — don't invent work.

### 3.3 Poke one-liners (tight, role-selected echoes)

These are the compressed forms injected at poke time. They are deliberate echoes of §3.1/§3.2, guarded against drift (see §5.3).

- **Manager poke line**:
  > *Your goal: drive your board + your workers' tasks to verified completion (receipts, not claims), then help peer managers with unowned work; manage don't build; when all clear, report and prepare for re-spin.*
- **Worker poke line**:
  > *Your goal: finish your assigned tasks, marking each done in the store with a receipt; escalate blockers, stay in your lane; empty list → signal ready-for-respin.*

## 4. What changed vs Rick's draft (the walkthrough)

| Rick's draft | My modification | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Manager: "chase all work… to completion" | + "to **verified** completion… each closed with a **receipt**" | No-confabulation rule — "done" must cite evidence, not a claim. |
| Manager: "then help fellow managers" | + "taking only **unowned or offered** items, never another manager's owned lane" | Prevents two managers stomping each other / cap + manifest collisions; mirror of MANAGE-NOT-BUILD. |
| (none) | + "When board clear and no peer needs help, **report + prepare for re-spin**" | Graceful idle — stops make-work / thrashing on an empty board. |
| Manager: "clear their task list" | + "Done = `task_query` owner+worker scope returns zero open" | Makes completion **machine-checkable**, hooks the owed-work oracle. |
| Worker: "finish all work… and marked as completed" (ambiguous) | "complete every assigned task, **transition each → done as you finish**, with a receipt" | Removes the logic knot; bakes store-status discipline into the goal. |
| (none) | + "**escalate blockers, stay in your lane**; empty → ready-for-respin" | Anti-absorption (worker-side mirror) + a clean termination signal. |

3. **Cross-link** `manager-autonomy.md` (§1) and `task-store-discipline.md` (§0) and `swe-team-roles.md` charters to point at `role-goals.md` as the "why."

### Phase 1 — Spin-up injection (planning-is-prompting)

### Phase 2 — Poke injection (lupin)

7. **Define the poke one-liners as runtime configuration-manager strings** (lupin-app.ini, Baseline section — e.g. `heartbeat manager goal line` / `heartbeat worker goal line`), read via the configuration_manager at poke time. This lets the wording be retuned live, **without a code change or deploy**. (Rick, 2026-06-24 — supersedes the original "named code constants" proposal.) A `*-splainer.ini` entry documents each key; a comment in the consuming poke code cites `planning-is-prompting → workflow/role-goals.md` as the canonical human-readable source.
8. **Site #2 + #3 (arbiter)** — role is already in `view`/`row`; append the role-selected goal line to `_format_poke()` and `_format_manager_stale_poke()`.
9. **Site #4 (cascade scheduler)** — manager-only; append `MANAGER_GOAL_LINE`.
10. **Site #1 (stop-hook self-poke)** — the hard one. Role isn't known. **Recommended**: inject the session's role into bridge metadata / the hold file at spawn time (manager vs worker is known at `spawn_sessions`), then read it in `_run_heartbeat()` and select the goal line. Fallback if role is absent: emit a **role-agnostic** goal line ("finish owed work to verified completion; escalate blockers; empty → ready-for-respin") so the poke degrades gracefully rather than guessing.

### Phase 3 — Drift guard + tests

11. **Drift guard** — a unit test in lupin asserting the constant one-liners contain the key clauses, plus a comment-anchor linking back to `role-goals.md`. (Full text lives in markdown; poke carries the compressed echo — two tiers, one guarded.)
12. **Smoke** — verify each poke site renders the goal line for the right role.
