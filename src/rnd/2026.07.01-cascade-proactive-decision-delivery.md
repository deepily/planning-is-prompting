## 1. What Rick flagged

During the live cascade, the Manager (Mr Radio) — and the Steward (María) in her status notifies — **deferentially buried the lead**: user-destined design decisions were parked behind a *"nothing needs you right now — it'll surface async at Step-9"* framing, and softly offered ("do you want to review?") rather than **driven to Rick's desk**. Rick's words:

> "Mr Radio deferentially buried the lead to walk me through the resulting design decisions… instead of plunging right into it like a good drive-a-process-to-completion manager would normally do. Can we tighten the workflow language such that the manager takes the findings and puts them in front of my face for review without asking if I want to review them?"

**Ratified cadence** (Rick, `ask_multiple_choice` 2026-07-01): **Proactive per-batch walkthrough.**

## 2. The design tension (what must NOT break)

The cascade's prime directive is **"save the user's attention"** — the Manager is a **filter**. This tightening must therefore be **scoped surgically**:

- **IN scope** — *user-destined decisions*: findings/OQs that genuinely need Rick's judgment (Layer-3 milestone challenges, scope-expansion forks, genuine product/preference OQs like V9 drop-vs-build, cross-section conflicts).
- **OUT of scope (still filtered, stays OFF his desk)** — manager-resolvable findings: cosmetic, within-group inconsistency, and factual/simplification corrections the group closes itself (today's B1 / V9-mechanics / D1 were correctly resolved author-side; only the *residual preference* — e.g. V9 drop-vs-build, OQ-1 relabel — is user-destined).

**The tightening changes the DELIVERY of user-destined decisions, not the filter.** It removes the *permission-ask* and the *deferral/burying*; it does not open the floodgates.

## 3. Chosen mechanism

When a **batch** of user-destined decisions firms up (per section as it closes, and at cascade-complete), the Manager **immediately drives a guided walkthrough** — the `/plan-decide` instrument: one decision at a time, each with **pros, cons, and an explicit recommendation**, in **descending priority**, via `ask_multiple_choice`. **No "do you want to review?" preamble. No parking into a buried Step-9 batch.**

### 4.1 `plan-review-cascaded.md` → Manager System Prompt (new meta-rule)

> **6. Default to proactive decision-delivery (added 2026-07-01, Rick post-game):** the moment a batch of *user-destined* decisions firms up — per section as it closes, and at cascade-complete — you MUST **drive them to the user proactively** via a guided decision-walkthrough (`/plan-decide`: one at a time, pros/cons/explicit recommendation, descending priority). You do NOT ask "do you want to review?"; you do NOT defer them into a buried Step-9 batch; you put them in front of the user directly. This does NOT relax meta-rule 1 (the filter): only decisions that genuinely need the user's judgment are driven to them — manager-resolvable findings (cosmetic / within-group inconsistency / factual-simplification) stay resolved within the group and OFF the user's desk. Burying a user-destined decision behind a "nothing needs you now" framing, or gating it behind a permission-ask, is a **redline** — the inverse of driving the process to completion.
