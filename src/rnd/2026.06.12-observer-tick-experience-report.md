## 2. What the ticks actually bought (the case FOR keep)

1. **The one genuine stall of the night was caught by a tick probe, not by a human** — S108 manager wedge: detected at the tick boundary, escalated with pane-state receipts, resolved by directed poke. Without the tick the wedge had no other observer.
2. **Structure, not just alarm**: each tick forced a disk-read probe of every active topic. In S109 this produced a ~5-min-grain audit trail of a fast-moving pipeline (boot→pass1→pass2→pass3→verdict→fold→delta-verify→staffed in ~25 min) that no one had to reconstruct afterward — the ack ledger IS the post-game record.
3. **Deadline pressure on the watcher (me)**: a tick owed = an ack owed = a probe done. The discipline half of the mechanism worked exactly as designed; I never drifted more than one cadence-interval from ground truth.
4. **Clean lifecycle**: both runs started with one command, exited themselves on the completion signal, and left a receipt. Zero zombie daemons.

## 3. What it cost (the case for TUNE)

1. **Delivery is the broken half (S108's ~44%)**: emission ≈ 100% but the wake-the-loop leg fails far too often (FM-18 family). Ticks then arrive BATCHED — by the time the observer wakes, 2–3 ticks of "freshness" have already expired. **The tick is only as good as its delivery path.**
2. **Quiet-tick noise at 180s (4:1)**: during cascade lulls (long author/review stages) most ticks probe an unchanged world. Each costs a full observer turn + an ack post. The S109 300s run had no noise problem — but the review was atypically fast; cadence alone doesn't solve it.
3. **Adaptive cadence is documented but not implemented** — both runs ran flat. The M=8/4/2 design exists only on paper; "honor adaptive cadence" (tentative tuning 2) is currently a manual `--cadence-active` choice.
4. **Poke-target blindness**: S108 pokes fired at sessions that COULD NOT respond (quota dialog, context-EOL). Without a pane-state differential the poke is theater against exactly the failure modes it should catch (tentative tuning 3 exists because of this).
5. **The daemon's own substrate is the debt**: my S108 ledger contains 2 `:7999` connection failures — the same saturation the task-store F2/LANDED gate now exists to fix. Self-referential evidence in favor of the LANDED pin.

## 4. Tunings assessment (against the tentative ruling's three)

| Tentative tuning | My experience-based verdict |
|---|---|
| (1) Delta-aware ticks (wake only on change/threshold) | **STRONGEST of the three.** Would have eliminated ~80% of S108's quiet ticks while losing nothing — every eventful tick involved new topic bytes, which is exactly the delta a disk-read scheduler can detect cheaply before dispatching the DM. The 60-tick ledger gives the spec: tick = silent unless (new content on watched topics) OR (strikes-threshold heartbeat, e.g. every Nth tick regardless, as a dead-man's floor). |
| (2) Honor adaptive cadence (M=8/4/2) | Real but second-order once (1) lands — delta-awareness makes cadence mostly a polling-cost knob, not a noise knob. Implement in the scheduler, not as a manual flag. |
| (3) Pane-state differential before poking | **Keep, already proven**: the S108 false-alarm withdrawal and the 3-mechanism differential both came from pane-state evidence. Codify the check the bug-queue amendment describes (rehydration window · quota freeze · deliberation latency are NOT stalls). |

**Additional tuning the ledger argues for (not in the tentative three): fix DELIVERY before anything else.** The ~44% failed-to-wake leg (FM-18) makes every other tuning secondary — a perfectly delta-aware, adaptive, differential-checked tick that doesn't wake the observer is still a silent tick. The long-frame answer is already ruled (detection half migrates to arbiter + task-store; observer-discipline half stays) — but until the arbiter consumes store-backed liveness, the tick daemon's DM-delivery leg deserves the first engineering hour.

## 5. Long-frame alignment (where this ends up)

Consistent with the ruled direction: the **detection** half (who moved, who stalled, what's owed) migrates to the `:8001` arbiter reading the unified task-store (now pouring, Phase 1); the **discipline** half (the observer owes a probe + a receipted ack on a clock) stays a practice. The tick daemon is the bridge mechanism until that lands — worth keeping WITH tuning (1) + the delivery fix, then retiring tick-by-tick as arbiter coverage arrives.

## 6. Recommendation (one paragraph, for the dig)

**KEEP-WITH-TUNINGS confirmed, with a re-ordered priority: delivery-fix first, delta-aware ticks second, pane-differential codified third, adaptive cadence last** (mostly subsumed by delta-awareness). The mechanism caught the night's only real stall and produced the audit trail the post-game is being written from; its costs are all in the delivery leg and the quiet-tick noise, both fixable without touching the practice. Retire progressively as the arbiter + task-store detection path comes online.
