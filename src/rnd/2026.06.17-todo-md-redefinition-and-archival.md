## 3. Branch-scoped archival automation (your ask B)

**Goal**: TODO.md should only ever show what's relevant to the **current + next-two** branch horizon; everything older auto-archives. This mirrors the proven `history.md` adaptive archival, but keyed on **branch/version horizon**, not pure age.

### 3.1 Retention rule

- **Keep in TODO.md**: items whose horizon ∈ { current branch, +1, +2 }.
- **Archive**: items tied to a branch/version that is already merged or > 2 ahead is past — moved to `todo-archive/<version-range>-todo.md` (e.g. `todo-archive/v0.1.0-to-v0.1.2-todo.md`).
- **Per-section behavior**:
  - **Decisions Log** → archives by **date** (like history.md: entries older than the retention window move out; greppable archive preserved).
  - **Backlog (`## Pending`)** → archives when its **owning branch horizon passes**.
  - **Completed (Recent)** → already 7-day windowed; archive on the same pass.
  - **Pending Decisions** → never auto-archived while open (a live decision queue); archived only once ruled (the ruling lands in the Decisions Log).

### 3.2 Mechanism (mirrors history-management)

- A **session-end health check** (added to `session-end.md`) measures TODO.md size + scans for past-horizon items → proposes an archive.
- A **`/plan-todo archive`** mode (new mode on the existing `/plan-todo` command) runs it on demand: detect current version (from branch name, e.g. `wip-v0.1.3-…`), compute the keep-horizon, move past-horizon sections/items to `todo-archive/`, leave a one-line breadcrumb (`→ archived to <file>`).
- **Naming**: `todo-archive/<vLow>-to-<vHigh>-todo.md` (version-range) or `todo-archive/YYYY-MM-DD-todo.md` (date, for the Decisions Log slice) — matching the history.md archive convention.

### 3.3 The one open question (needs your call)

**How does an item get associated with a branch horizon?** Items today aren't branch-tagged. Options:

1. **(Recommended) Lightweight `horizon:` tag, default-current.** An item may carry `| horizon: v0.1.3` (like the existing `| priority:` / `| raised:` tags). **Untagged = current horizon → kept.** Archival only moves items explicitly tagged to a past version OR (for the Decisions Log) older than the date window. Pro: zero burden for normal use; opt-in precision; backward-compatible (nothing breaks day one). Con: relies on tagging items you *want* to age out early.
2. **Pure date-based** (like history.md, ignore branches). Pro: fully automatic, no tags. Con: ignores your explicit "branch horizon" framing — a still-relevant old item ages out by calendar.
3. **Section-stamped horizon.** Group `## Pending` under `### Horizon vX.Y` subheheaders; archive whole subsections when the horizon passes. Pro: visually obvious. Con: heavier restructure of the current flat list.

*(My recommendation: **Option 1** — it honors your branch-horizon intent, stays automatic for the common case via default-current, and degrades gracefully.)*
