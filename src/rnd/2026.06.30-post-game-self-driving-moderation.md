## 1. The diagnosis — a missing role, not a missing step

`post-game.md` (v1.0) names the **Workflow Steward** as the owner of the *synthesis* (§1: "owned by the Workflow Steward"). What it never names is a **moderator of the participant *conversation***. §3 lists the inputs as *receipts to gather* (git, store, commons, tests, ledger) — a one-way pull — and the full-retro template (§4) is a *document to write*. Nowhere does the workflow describe **who drives the multi-party discussion** when a run had multiple participants (a fleet of managers, a SWE crew, a cascade panel).

With that role unspecified, last night it defaulted to the highest-authority human in the room — **Rick**. The actual shape that emerged:

```
   participant ─┐
   participant ─┼──▶  RICK (moderator)  ──relay──▶  María (collator)  ──▶ doc
   participant ─┘
```

Rick drove the conversation; each participant relayed their went-well / didn't / do-better notes **to Rick**; María only collated what Rick forwarded. That inverts the intended division of labor. The Steward owns the retrospective; the user is its **audience and final approver**, not its switchboard.

The target shape:

```
                 ┌──────────────  María (MODERATOR)  ──────────────┐
   participant ◀─┤  solicits · names threads · cross-examines · synthesizes  ├─▶ doc + rulings
   participant ◀─┤                                                 │
   participant ◀─┘                  transcript ▼ (tail + digests)
                                          RICK (audience — intervenes only at gates)
```

This is the same **role-separation** principle the `TEST OWNERSHIP MANDATE` already encodes elsewhere: the human *designs and uses*; Claude *executes the labor*. Here: the human *listens and ratifies*; the Steward *runs the conversation*.

## 2. The proposed change to `post-game.md`

Add **one new section** (proposed §3.5, between the current §3 "Inputs" and §4 "Template") plus a short **audience-contract** callout. Drop-in text follows.

### 2.1 New section — drop-in for `post-game.md`

> ### 3.5 The moderated participant roundtable (multi-participant runs)
>
> When the engagement had **more than one participant** (a fleet of managers, a SWE crew, a cascade panel), the post-game is not a solo write-up — it is a **conversation the Steward moderates**. The Steward owns driving it end-to-end; **the user is the audience, never the moderator or the relay.**
>
> **Moderator = the Workflow Steward.** In a solo run with no Steward, the session moderates its own single-participant roundtable (which collapses to the §1 self-report + the D3 user-approval gate).
>
> **The protocol (4 beats):**
>
> 1. **Open the floor (solicit directly).** The Steward posts a structured retro prompt to the commons `post-game` topic — the four-field frame: *went-well · didn't · do-better · receipts*. Every participant posts their own contribution **to the topic**, addressed to the Steward — **not to the user.** Reaped workers are briefly respun from their memento, or contribute via their last memento if a respin isn't warranted (mark such entries `from-memento`).
> 2. **Name the threads.** The Steward reads all contributions and posts a **convergence summary** to the topic: the common threads, the disagreements, the still-open questions. (Last night's real example: all three managers independently misread the same wind-down instruction — a thread only visible once the contributions sit side by side.)
> 3. **Run ≥1 cross-examination round (the actual conversation).** The Steward drives discussion **persona-to-persona** in-topic: *"Mr Radio and Tiberius both named the wind-down miss — concur on the root cause? Tiberius, you called it 'weak agency' — is that the same failure as Mr Radio's 'caution-as-deferral'?"* Participants answer **to the topic**. The Steward keeps driving rounds until the threads converge or are explicitly logged as unresolved. **This is the transcript the user follows.**
> 4. **Synthesize + close the floor.** The Steward writes the §4 doc from the converged transcript and produces §5 movement. Participants are thanked and released (reaped with mementos per harvest discipline).
>
> **The user's role across all four beats is to LISTEN** (see the audience contract below) — and to act **only** at the genuine gates: the D3 self-review approval (solo) and the doctrine-graduation approval (§5.3 / `accumulate-pattern-before-graduating`).

### 2.2 New callout — the audience contract

> **The "Rick = audience" contract.** In a moderated roundtable the user is an **observer with a veto at the gates**, not a participant in the mechanics. Concretely:
> - The Steward **never routes a participant's contribution through the user.** Contributions go participant → topic → Steward.
> - The transcript **streams to the user passively**: he can tail the commons `post-game` topic live, and the Steward fires a **notify-abstract digest** at two beats — after the convergence summary (beat 2) and at synthesis (beat 4) — so an AFK user can follow without reading raw commons. (Honor the `notify`-on-attention-demanding-mode mandate: cross-session dialogue is invisible to the user unless surfaced.)
> - The user is pulled in **only at a gate** — never to moderate, relay, or keep the conversation moving. "Keep it moving" is the Steward's job by definition.
> - If the user *does* interject mid-roundtable, the Steward folds it in as one more contribution and **resumes moderating** — it does not hand the baton back.

## 3. The one real fork — how the rounds physically run

All three options below implement the same contract (Steward moderates, user listens). They differ only in the **transport** for the conversation.

| Option | Mechanism | Pro | Con |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Commons roundtable** ✅ *recommended* | Steward posts to the `post-game` topic; participants post back; cross-exam rounds happen in-topic; user tails the topic. | Reuses the **exact mechanism Rick ratified last night**; genuine multi-party; produces a clean, linear, human-readable transcript by construction. | Async; participants must be live or briefly respun to answer follow-ups. |
| **B. DM interview star** | Steward DMs each participant a structured retro, collects replies, converges privately, publishes the synthesis. | Simplest; works even when workers are already reaped (memento-backed); cheap. | It's an *interview*, not a *conversation* — no participant-to-participant exchange, thinner transcript. Doesn't match "follow the conversation." |
| **C. Live facilitated panel** | Steward keeps all participants live and relays back-and-forth until convergence. | Richest, most genuinely conversational. | Most expensive; needs every session live concurrently; highest coordination overhead. |

**✅ RATIFIED: A** (Rick, 2026-06-30). It is the mechanism already proven and ratified in last night's run, it yields exactly the "transcript I follow along" Rick asked for, and it **scales down gracefully** — a solo run is just a one-participant roundtable (the session interviewing itself, then the D3 user gate). B is the automatic fallback when participants are already reaped and a respin isn't worth it (the Steward can mix: live participants converse in-topic, reaped ones contribute `from-memento`). C is reserved for high-stakes retros where live cross-talk is worth the cost.

## 7. The post-collection digest ping (Rick, 2026-06-30)

A **mandatory digest gate** between collection and synthesis. After beat 1 (all participants' contributions are in) and beat 2 (the Steward's convergence summary), the Steward fires **one `notify()` to Rick** with:
- `message` (spoken, ≤3 sentences): the headline — *"All N contributions in; here's the through-line."*
- `abstract` (the card): a **doc-viewer link** to the in-progress transcript/synthesis + a tight summary of *what we've seen* — the common threads, the disagreements, the open questions.

This is the moment Rick "catches up on the transcript" without having to tail commons live. It is **a digest, not a gate** — the Steward keeps moderating the cross-examination rounds (beat 3) without waiting; Rick reads async and only interrupts if he wants to steer. (A second digest fires at synthesis/beat 4 per §2.2.) Fold this into §2.1 beat 2→3 and the §2.2 audience contract as the **"after-collection digest"** obligation.

## 8. Tracking the conversations over time — the post-game corpus (Rick, 2026-06-30)

**Requirement:** these conversations must "live in their own directory and be reviewed, researched, and drawn on ad hoc as we make plans to update or integrate them into our workflows." Today they're scattered across `src/rnd/` with mixed `-postgame` / `-post-game` suffixes (~12 of them) — not a browsable corpus.

**Proposed design — a dedicated, indexed, researchable corpus:**

1. **Directory:** `io/post-games/` — a single home, **gitignored and local-only** (Rick, 2026-06-30: *"store the postgames in io/post-games … that way it doesn't get tracked by the repo"*). Post-games are research artifacts, not committed doctrine; this keeps them out of version control (sibling to `io/mementos/`). Doc-viewability is preserved by adding the `io/post-games/` prefix to `.docview.yml` (same precedent as `io/git-loc-delta/`) — needs a lupin-rest-dev bounce to take effect.
2. **Naming (standardized):** `yyyy.mm.dd-<slug>-post-game.md` — one canonical suffix (`-post-game`, retiring the `-postgame` variant going forward).
3. **The transcript is preserved, not just the synthesis.** Each entry's §4 doc embeds (or links a sibling `…-transcript.md` for) the actual roundtable exchange — so the corpus is *researchable conversation*, not only conclusions.
4. **An index — `io/post-games/README.md`** — the registry that makes it researchable: a table of every post-game with `date · engagement · type · key threads/tags · rulings · graduation-status`. **Tags are the cross-time tracking substrate**: a recurring tag (e.g. `drive-vs-hold`, `confabulation`, `wind-down-misread`) across N entries is exactly the *"larger cross-session pattern"* the `accumulate-pattern-before-graduating-doctrine` rule waits for before graduating a lesson. The corpus + tag-index operationalizes that rule.
5. **Relocation of the 11 existing post-games — ✅ DONE** (Rick, 2026-06-30, two-step: *"why are you not driving … into their own directory?"* → then *"store them in io/post-games … that way it doesn't get tracked by the repo"*). All 11 actual post-game retros were moved into `io/post-games/` **and removed from git tracking** — their prior committed `src/rnd/` copies are staged for deletion (removed on the next commit); the files persist locally as untracked, gitignored artifacts. Every reference in the tracked docs was repathed to `io/post-games/<f>` across README, history, history-archive, todo-archive, workflow docs, and executive-briefings in the same pass — verified no dangling refs (the `sam-tts-storm` **postmortem** is NOT a post-game and correctly stayed in `src/rnd/`). The `2026.06.30` proposal seed doc itself stays tracked in `src/rnd/` (it's a proposal, not a retro). HELD for commit.

   **Consequence (flagged):** the committed doctrine docs (README, workflow, history) now link to `io/post-games/` paths that are **local-only** — those links resolve on this machine but not on a fresh clone. This is the inherent trade-off of an untracked corpus; accepted per Rick's explicit choice. Doc-viewer links remain valid once the `.docview.yml` change is picked up by a lupin-rest-dev bounce.

**Going-forward default (regardless of migration choice):** every new post-game is written into `io/post-games/` and registered in its `README.md` index with tags. The workflow §4 "write to `src/rnd/yyyy.mm.dd-<slug>-post-game.md`" updates to `io/post-games/…`.
