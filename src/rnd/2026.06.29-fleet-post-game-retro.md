## 1. THE COMMON THREAD — drive-vs-hold miscalibration

Independently, the managers' misses converge on **one root failure**: *mis-reading WHEN to drive silently vs WHEN to hold/surface.* It shows up as two **symmetric** faces:

- **Over-surface / over-hold-UP** — surfacing or interrupting when the right move was to proceed silently. (María: fired a `converse()` while Rick was mid-read of the plan; earlier buried-then-re-fired a gate.)
- **Under-drive / over-defer-DOWN** — deferring / checkpointing / narrowing / parking / **resting** when a mechanism to just proceed already exists, or the instruction was plainly "finish." (María: read the wind-down as "stop/rest," wrote a hold literally saying *Resting*. Mr Radio: deferred F0-staffing on a "types.ts hotspot" rationale that worktree isolation already solved; told Cheech to *checkpoint* F0 when Rick said *finish*. Tiberius: parked Krishna + re-queued his P1 instead of finishing; asked permission to staff a job plainly his to drive; hid behind a self-imposed chase-timer as a "deadline.")

Both faces are the **same judgment error**: substituting a *felt* sense — "this needs surfacing" / "this is too big, defer" — for the **actual signal**: the mechanism that already exists, or the instruction as plainly stated.

**Strongest evidence this is the real thread:** *all three managers independently misread the same wind-down instruction the same way* — "finish your work" → stop (María: "resting") / checkpoint (Mr Radio: park Cheech's F0) / park (Tiberius: parked Krishna + re-queued his P1). **Three-for-three on the identical instruction** — a fleet-wide convergence, not an individual slip. And each independently traced it to the *same* root (drive-vs-defer calibration), naming it from a different face: María "autonomy-calibration," Mr Radio "caution-as-deferral," Tiberius "park-and-defer instead of drive-and-own (strong on craft, weak on agency)."
